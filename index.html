Short Questions (30+)

Q1: Define gaseous exchange.
Ans: Process of exchanging oxygen and carbon dioxide between organism and environment.

Q2: Main organ of gaseous exchange in humans?
Ans: Lungs

Q3: What is alveolus?
Ans: Tiny air sac in lungs where oxygen and carbon dioxide exchange occurs.

Long Questions

Q1: Describe the mechanism of breathing in humans.
Ans: Inhalation: diaphragm contracts → thoracic cavity volume increases → lungs expand → air enters. Exhalation: diaphragm relaxes → thoracic cavity volume decreases → lungs compress → air exits.

Q2: Explain the transport of oxygen and carbon dioxide in blood.
Ans: Oxygen binds to haemoglobin and is transported to tissues. Carbon dioxide is transported as bicarbonate ions (~70%), dissolved CO₂ (~7%), carbamino compounds (~23%).

Q3: Compare gaseous exchange in plants and humans.
Ans: Humans: alveoli, lungs; Plants: stomata, lenticels, root hairs. Humans: ventilation required; Plants: diffusion sufficient.
